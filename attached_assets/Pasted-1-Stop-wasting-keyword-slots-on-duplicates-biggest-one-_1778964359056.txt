1. Stop wasting keyword slots on duplicates (biggest one)

Apple combines tokens across name + subtitle + keyword field, so any word that appears in the name or subtitle should be removed from the keyword field. Your current 100-char field repeats relocation and planner — both already in the subtitle — and expat is in the app name.

Also: don't use spaces after commas, and split phrases into single words. Apple auto-combines tokens, so "move abroad" wastes 6 chars vs "move,abroad" which ranks for both individually plus the phrase. Same for "digital nomad", "retire abroad".

A rewrite of the keyword field (100 chars, no spaces):

visa,golden,d7,nomad,citizenship,passport,emigrate,overseas,live,country,quiz,tax,move,gap,year

That's ~92 chars and gets you ranked for combinations like "golden visa", "d7 visa", "live overseas", "nomad visa", "tax abroad", "gap year", "move overseas" — terms you currently miss entirely.

2. Use the subtitle for keywords the name doesn't already cover

Current: Relocation guides & planning (28 chars, ranks for "relocation", "guides", "planning")

Stronger: Visa, residency & moving plan (29 chars — adds visa, residency, moving as ranking terms; drops "guides" which is low-volume)

Or, country-pitched: Visa & residency move planner (29 chars)

3. Tighten the app name to lead with a real search term

ExpatHub - Move Abroad Smarter (30 chars) burns 6 chars on "Smarter" which nobody searches for.

Try: ExpatHub: Move Abroad & Visa (28 chars) — keeps brand, adds "visa" as a ranking term in the highest-weight field. Or ExpatHub: Relocate Overseas (27 chars) if you want broader reach.

4. Front-load the description

Apple shows only the first ~3 lines before "more". Your current opening — "Moving to another country is one of the biggest decisions you'll ever make" — is brand-y but doesn't tell the user what the app does. Lead with the concrete promise:

Take the 2-minute Country Fit Quiz and get a personalised readiness score, your top destination match, and a clear list of what's blocking your move — across 11 countries.

(Doesn't affect ranking, but lifts conversion, which Apple uses as a ranking signal.)

Medium-effort lifts
5. Add localized storefronts to double your keyword field

App Store indexes keyword fields per locale. By adding English (UK) and English (Australia) alongside English (US), you get three independent 100-char keyword fields — Apple ranks the user against all of them. Costs ~10 min to set up in App Store Connect, no translation needed (the copy is already in English). This is the single biggest free ASO win for an English-only app.

6. In-app events

App Store Connect lets you publish "In-App Events" (e.g., "Country Fit Quiz refresh", "New: Australia pathway"). Events appear on your product page and in search, and they're indexed. You can run up to 10 active at a time.

7. Custom product pages

You can create up to 35 alternate product pages with different screenshots, preview videos, and promo text, then deep-link to each from Apple Search Ads or Meta. Useful if you want to A/B a "digital nomad" angle vs a "retire abroad" angle without changing the default listing.

8. Screenshots & preview video

The single biggest conversion lever. If your current screenshots are device frames with feature labels, consider testing: large headline text + a hero benefit per screenshot (e.g., "Find your country in 2 minutes" / "Compare 11 destinations on 14 factors" / "Plan your move step by step"). Apple Search Ads + Product Page Optimization can A/B these for you.
